The objective of this project is to illustrate the tendency of musical features to produce songs to be 'hits' based on music components. This will support senior management in deciding on investing to produce songs based on the dynamics of songs and elements by considering tempo, genre, danceability, duration of the track and explicit in order to generate financial benefit in terms of revenue including profitability of the company.

The dashboard will consist of musical features that affect the song to hit as following factors.
- Genre: genre of songs such as pop, rock, hip-hop, etc.
- Tempo: it is the speed or pace of a given piece which is measured by beats per minute or BPM.
- Duration of track: duration of track in minutes
- Danceability: the ability to dance along with the track, considering from combination of tempo, rhythm, beat, and overall regularity which is measured by a value of 0.0 - 1.0. The least danceable is 0.0 and the most danceable is 1.0.
- Explicit: the content of a song is not suitable for children, or it could be offensive for some content.

To achieve popularity, there are many musical features to influence songs to hit such as tempo, genre, danceability, explicit, and duration of track.
According to the research from the University of Bristol (2011), the popularity of songs can be predicted which a hit potential is measured by a score and there are some trends of musical features that are related to popularity. For instance, popular songs are likely to be danceable after the eighties. Also, ballad songs that have a tempo of 70-89 bpm. (beats per minute) tended to gain popularity in the eighties. Moreover, the duration of the song also affects the algorithm in the streaming platform. Due to the skip rate, listeners tend to have fewer skipped on the short length of the song which will be prioritized by the platform (Leight, 2022). And that will affect the ranking of hit songs on the streaming platform. Furthermore, explicit songs are likely to have the potential to gain popularity. According to the record in 2017, the top 8 ranking albums in Apple Music's New Music section were full of explicit songs as well as Billboard chart had explicit songs number one ranking on chart such as Migos by Bad and Boujee, and Starboy by The Weekend (Ross, 2017).
